How our story will go...

Explore the timeline of events that'll go on during the hackathon

Registration
The registration process for a hackathon typically begins with participants visiting the event's official website or platform, where they can find detailed information about the event, including the theme, schedule, and rules. Participants are usually required to create an account or log in if they already have one. Once logged in, they need to fill out a registration form, providing personal details such as name, email address, and, if applicable, team information. Some hackathons may ask for additional details, such as a brief description of the participant's skills or a summary of their project idea. After submitting the form, participants usually receive a confirmation email, which may include further instructions or important dates. Depending on the hackathon, there might be a selection process where organizers review registrations and select participants based on specific criteria. Once accepted, participants are officially registered and can begin preparing for the event.
Inauguration & track selection
The track selection process for a hackathon involves participants choosing a specific focus area or theme within the event, often aligned with their skills or interests. After registering for the hackathon, participants are typically presented with various tracks, each representing different challenges, industries, or technologies. They can review detailed descriptions of each track, which might include the goals, potential projects, and judging criteria. Once familiar with the options, participants select their preferred track by clicking on the corresponding option, often through their registration account or event portal. Some hackathons allow participants to change tracks before the event starts, while others lock in choices at the time of selection. Upon choosing a track, participants may receive additional resources or guidelines related to their chosen focus area, helping them prepare their projects and align their efforts with the track's objectives.
Commencement of Hackathon
The hackathon kicks off with an opening ceremony, where organizers welcome participants, introduce the event's theme, and outline the schedule. This is followed by a briefing on the rules and expectations, after which participants dive straight into brainstorming and development. The energy is high as teams eagerly begin their creative journey, ready to tackle the challenges ahead.
Addressing Sessions
During the hackathon, several sessions are held to guide and support participants. These sessions include expert-led workshops, where participants can learn new skills or deepen their knowledge in specific areas, and mentoring sessions, where teams receive personalized advice and feedback on their projects. There are also check-in meetings to ensure teams are on track and to address any questions or concerns. These sessions are designed to enhance the overall hackathon experience, providing valuable insights and fostering collaboration.
Networking
Networking is a vital aspect of the hackathon experience, offering participants the opportunity to connect with like-minded individuals, industry experts, and potential collaborators. Throughout the event, participants can engage in informal conversations, join virtual or in-person networking sessions, and participate in team-building activities. These interactions not only help in building professional relationships but also open doors to future collaborations, job opportunities, and mentorship, making the hackathon a valuable platform for personal and career growth.
Project Presentaion
Project presentations mark the culmination of the hackathon, where teams showcase their innovative solutions to a panel of judges. This is the moment to highlight key features, demonstrate functionality, and explain the impact of their work, aiming to impress and secure recognition.
Project Evaluation
The jury evaluates each project based on criteria such as innovation, technical execution, and alignment with the hackathon's theme. They carefully assess the presentations, providing feedback and ultimately selecting the winning teams based on the project's overall quality and potential impact.
Closing and Award Ceremony
The hackathon concludes with a closing and award ceremony, where organizers reflect on the event's highlights and celebrate participants' achievements. Winners are announced, with prizes awarded to the top teams, followed by words of encouragement and future opportunities. This ceremony serves as a final moment of recognition and community before the event officially wraps up.

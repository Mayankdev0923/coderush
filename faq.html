FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

What should I bring to Hackathon?

keyboard_arrow_down

Participants should bring a laptop, charger, any necessary software or tools, and personal items like snacks or a water bottle. We will provide meals, snacks, and other essentials, but it’s always good to come prepared.

Who can participate in the hackathon?

keyboard_arrow_down

CodeRush is open to everyone, from beginners to experienced developers, designers, and entrepreneurs. Your skills and interest might be different from our themes and tracks. Check our brochure for more.

Do I need team to participate?

keyboard_arrow_down

Yes, candidates are encouraged to apply for the hackathon as teams. It is recommended that teams consist of 3 to 5 members to ensure an efficient and smooth workflow throughout the event.

What if I don't have any coding experience?

keyboard_arrow_down

CodeRush is beginner-friendly and encourages participants to learn as they go. Workshops and mentors are often available to help you get started. Don’t be afraid to ask for help or work on the design, marketing, or ideation aspects if you’re not comfortable with coding.

How much time do we have to complete our project?

keyboard_arrow_down

The time frame for a hackathon is 24 hours. Make sure to plan your time effectively, focusing on core features first and adding extras if time allows.

What are the judging criteria?

keyboard_arrow_down

Judging criteria often include innovation, technical difficulty, user experience, impact, and completeness of the project. Some hackathons may also have specific criteria related to the event’s theme.

Will there be mentors available?

keyboard_arrow_down

We will provide access to mentors who can offer guidance on technical challenges, project direction, or pitching your idea. Don’t hesitate to reach out to them if you’re stuck or need advice. There too will be addressing sessions for teams.

Can I work on an existing project?

keyboard_arrow_down

CodeRush requires that all work be done during the event. However, you can bring pre-existing tools, libraries, and frameworks as long as the core project is developed during the hackathon.

What if I need a break?

keyboard_arrow_down

Taking breaks is important to stay fresh and focused. We have designated rest areas, and you’re encouraged to step away and recharge when needed.

What happens after the hackathon?

keyboard_arrow_down

After the hackathon, you may be asked to present your project to judges or a larger audience. Prizes are usually awarded, and many participants continue developing their projects post-event. Networking and connecting with other participants can also lead to future collaborations.

How do I get my prize if I win?

keyboard_arrow_down

Prizes will be distributed at the end of the event during a closing ceremony. We may arrange to send prizes digitally.
